Bhagavad Gītā

Chapter 2 · Sānkhya Yoga

कर्मण्येवाधिकारस्ते मा फलेषु कदाचन ।
मा कर्मफलहेतुर्भूर्मा ते सङ्गोऽस्त्वकर्मणि ॥
karmaṇy-evādhikāras te mā phaleṣu kadācana
mā karma-phala-hetur bhūr mā te saṅgo 'stv akarmaṇi
VERSE 2.47
Translation

You have a right to perform your prescribed duty, but you are not entitled to the fruits of your actions. Never consider yourself the cause of the results of your activities, and never be attached to not doing your duty.

Commentary

This śloka is the heart of Karma Yoga — the path of selfless action. Lord Krishna counsels Arjuna to act with full devotion yet release all anxiety about the outcome. When the mind is freed from the craving for results, action becomes pure, focus becomes absolute, and the heart grows steady amid success and failure alike.

In the light of jyotiṣa, the planets reveal our inherited tendencies and karmic rhythms — but it is through conscious, dedicated effort that we move gracefully through them. Liberation is not the absence of action; it is the transformation of our relationship to it. Do your dharma, offer the fruit to the Divine, and walk without fear.
